🏗️ Data Center Pause Extended 60 Days

🏗️ Data Center Pause Extended 60 Days

The Gist: The Augusta Commission voted unanimously Tuesday to extend its moratorium on new data centers by 60 days. A proposed 90-day extension failed 5-5 before commissioners agreed to add public hearings at the Warren Road, Diamond Lakes and Henry Brigham community centers.

Why It Matters: The delay gives residents and city staff more time to examine noise, water use, utility demand and buffers before Augusta adopts its first permanent data center rules.

What's Next: The moratorium now runs through mid-September. City staff will set dates for the three public hearings, while commissioners plan to tour a QTS facility on Aug. 3.

🩸 Local Blood Supply Hits Emergency Level

🩸 Local Blood Supply Hits Emergency Level

The Gist: Shepeard Community Blood Center says it has exhausted supplies of O-positive, O-negative, B-positive and A-negative blood, types used by two-thirds of patients who need transfusions. The center can meet only half of local hospitals' requests and has left some Children's Hospital of Georgia orders unfilled.

Why It Matters: The shortage affects trauma care, critically ill newborns and other patients across the CSRA. It is Shepeard's first emergency donation appeal in more than two years.

What's Next: All Shepeard donor centers in the CSRA are open daily this week, including Saturday. The center is asking donors of all blood types to give as soon as possible.

🏥 Columbia County's First Hospital Sets Opening

🏥 Columbia County's First Hospital Sets Opening

The Gist: Wellstar says Columbia County's first hospital is scheduled to open Aug. 26 at 5000 Gateway Boulevard in Grovetown. The new facility will offer emergency care, robotic surgery and telehealth capabilities.

Why It Matters: A full-service hospital in fast-growing Columbia County gives residents a closer option for emergency and specialty care instead of sending every patient toward Augusta's existing hospitals.

What's Next: Wellstar plans a public open house on Aug. 8, with additional tour details still to come.

⚡ SRS Chosen for 1-Gigawatt Data Center Talks

⚡ SRS Chosen for 1-Gigawatt Data Center Talks

The Gist: The National Nuclear Security Administration selected Amentum to negotiate a lease for an AI data center at Savannah River Site. The proposal calls for a 1-gigawatt facility supported by roughly 2 gigawatts of on-site power, starting with natural gas and later shifting to nuclear energy.

Why It Matters: A project of this scale could reshape energy production, federal land use and development around a major Augusta-area employer. Its on-site generation is intended to avoid raising existing customers' electric bills.

What's Next: Selection for talks does not guarantee construction. A final lease depends on negotiations, environmental and safety reviews, security evaluations, permits and other federal approvals.

🏠 Tiny-Home Community Opens Applications

🏠 Tiny-Home Community Opens Applications

The Gist: Bridge Builder Communities is accepting applications for Augusta's first tiny-home community, built for former foster youth ages 18 to 24. All homes are standing, the community building is about 85% complete, and residents will receive furnished housing, counseling and life-skills support while working or attending school.

Why It Matters: Young adults leaving foster care often lose housing and support at the same time. This program pairs a place to live with education, career planning and an individual path toward independence.

What's Next: The organization plans to welcome its first five residents at a mid-October ribbon cutting, then add residents gradually. Residency applications are open now.
