🍣 A Sushi Kitchen and a Hidden Whisky Bar on Broad

Located on Broad Street in downtown Augusta, 990 Broad Sushi Kitchen is a sleek sushi and crudo concept from Chef Ito in the former Pho-Ramen'L space.

Tucked behind the dining room sits The Ph'rog Bar, a hidden cocktail lounge with sake, Japanese whisky, and a polished late-night feel. The nigiri photo alone earns the click.

🏥 Fort Gordon Hospital Diverts Inpatient Critical Care

Eisenhower Army Medical Center is temporarily pausing inpatient critical care services because of staffing shortages. The emergency room remains open 24/7, and other clinical services remain available.

Why it matters: Fort Gordon families, retirees, and anyone who uses Eisenhower need to know what care is actually available before they need it.

What's next: Patients who need inpatient hospital care may be transferred into the local network while the hospital works through staffing.

Read more here.

🏗️ QTS Data Center Questions Keep Building

QTS released a new statement as Haynes Station neighbors continue pressing for answers about the planned data center near Fort Gordon. The company says the project could bring about 1,000 construction jobs over time and 160 to 220 permanent jobs once operational.

Why it matters: This is the local tradeoff in one project: jobs and tax base on one side, traffic, noise, wetlands, and neighborhood trust on the other.

What's next: Commissioners said another public meeting will happen after the May 19 primary election.

Read more here.

🏟️ Augusta Arena Still On Schedule

The Coliseum Authority says the new Augusta arena is still on schedule and on budget, with first-level wall framing now going up. Leaders are already talking about hotels and other businesses eyeing the area around the project.

Why it matters: A working arena could reshape downtown demand for restaurants, hotels, parking, and event-night foot traffic.

What's next: Seventh Street is expected to stay closed until July or August, and the Bell parking lot closes during June and July.

Read more here.

🏅 Columbia County Sports Complex Moves Forward

Columbia County commissioners approved $1.6 million for design work on a planned sports complex near the new hospital. The larger project is estimated around $30 million and is meant to draw youth tournaments.

Why it matters: Tournament sports can quietly change weekend hotel demand, restaurant traffic, and where families spend Saturdays.

Read more here.

🚁 Those Low Helicopters Were Marine Training

If you noticed extra military aircraft over Augusta, you were not imagining it. A Fort Gordon spokeswoman confirmed the U.S. Marine Corps is conducting helicopter training here, including CH-53K King Stallions.

Why it matters: Low aircraft near Daniel Field sparked enough local chatter that the simple explanation is useful.

Read more here.
