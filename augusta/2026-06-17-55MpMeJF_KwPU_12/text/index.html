🍖 Pit-Cooked Barbecue Off Bobby Jones

Sconyers Bar-B-Que

Off Bobby Jones Expressway at 2250 Sconyers Way, Sconyers Bar-B-Que has been an Augusta barbecue fixture since 1956. The old-fashioned pit operation is built around ribs, pork, chicken, tenderloin, turkey, hash and rice, and a secret family recipe.

Part of the lore is Larry Sconyers taking the barbecue to Washington in 1980 to cook on the White House lawn for President Jimmy Carter, members of Congress, and guests. Back home, the reclining pig sign still points locals toward plates, sauce, and tea.

🗳️ Kendrick Wins Augusta Mayoral Runoff

🗳️ Kendrick Wins Augusta Mayoral Runoff

The Gist: Steven Kendrick defeated incumbent Mayor Garnett Johnson in Tuesday's Augusta mayoral runoff, winning 54.3% of the vote with 14,651 votes to Johnson's 45.7% with 12,347. In Augusta Commission District 8, Evett Williams defeated Michael Cioffi 52.6% to 47.4%.

Why It Matters: Augusta's next mayor will inherit major decisions on growth, city finances, public safety, neighborhood trust and how the consolidated government handles big projects.

What's Next: Kendrick said his immediate priorities include an operational audit, a listening tour and getting the city's financial house in order.

🏥 VA Pipe Burst Damages Three Floors

🏥 VA Pipe Burst Damages Three Floors

The Gist: A pipe burst Friday evening at the downtown VA Medical Center, causing extensive weekend damage to parts of three floors. VA Augusta said the affected areas were primarily outpatient clinics, and all staff and patients in those areas were safely relocated.

Why It Matters: The downtown VA is a core health-care site for local veterans, so damaged clinic space can quickly affect appointments, access and where patients have to go for care.

What's Next: VA Augusta said restoration work is continuing, and affected patients have been contacted to reschedule or coordinate care through a community health-care partner.

💊 AmbioPharm Plans $118.96M Expansion

💊 AmbioPharm Plans $118.96M Expansion

The Gist: North Augusta-based AmbioPharm Inc. announced a $118.96 million Aiken County expansion that is expected to create 202 new jobs. The peptide manufacturer plans to build a modular manufacturing building as part of the project.

Why It Matters: A nearly $119 million life-sciences expansion adds high-value manufacturing jobs to the Augusta area and strengthens North Augusta's role in the region's medical and pharmaceutical economy.

What's Next: Operations are expected to be online by the end of 2027, and the company is directing interested applicants to its careers page.

🏀 North Augusta Funds Park Upgrades

🏀 North Augusta Funds Park Upgrades

The Gist: North Augusta City Council authorized about $300,000 for infrastructure improvements at Maude Edenfield Park and the North Augusta Community Center. The work includes repaving the parking lot and adding LED lighting at one of the city's busiest parks.

Why It Matters: The park and community center are heavily used by families, basketball players and summer recreation crowds, so better lighting and pavement are basic quality-of-life and safety upgrades.

🩺 Free Clinic Adds Monthly Appointments

🩺 Free Clinic Adds Monthly Appointments

The Gist: Empower Health Clinic is now operating with student volunteers from the Medical College of Georgia under ALAS, a nonprofit that provides no-cost health care for underserved families. The clinic moved from years of mobile screenings into a permanent facility with expanded services, diagnostic tools and referrals to AU Health labs.

Why It Matters: For uninsured and underinsured Augustans, a monthly no-cost clinic can mean blood pressure checks, blood sugar checks and physician access before a problem turns into an emergency-room visit.

What's Next: The clinic said July 25 appointments are available, the mobile clinic is still operating, and donations help support patient care.
