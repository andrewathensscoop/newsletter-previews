🥙 Shawarma, Falafel, and Fresh Pita in the Rotation

With a downtown Augusta spot and Evans roots, Laziza Mediterranean Grill is the easy Lebanese stop for shawarma, kebab plates, falafel, hummus, bowls, wraps, and warm pita.

The menu works for regular use: quick lunch, early dinner, family takeout, or a catering tray that feels brighter than sandwiches. Sauces and sides do a lot of the work here.

🏊 Pool Access Questions Keep Coming

The Gist: The Augusta Aquatics Center has been closed to regular members for nearly a month because of a safety-system issue. WRDW found that one paying group has still been allowed to use the pool while members wait.

Why It Matters: People are still paying for access they cannot use, and the exception makes a facility repair feel like a fairness problem.

What's Next: City officials still need to explain the repair timeline and how public members will be handled while the pool stays limited.

Read more here.

🏥 Eisenhower Downgrade Questions Stack Up

The Gist: The Defense Health Agency has confirmed plans to move Eisenhower Army Medical Center toward an ambulatory care model. The shift is drawing more questions from local leaders, military families, and retirees around Fort Gordon.

Why It Matters: Eisenhower is not just another clinic. It supports a large military and retiree patient base that depends on local access for more than routine appointments.

What's Next: Watch for congressional pressure and a clearer transition timeline as local officials push DHA for specifics.

Read more here.

🗳️ Early Voting Is Already Moving

The Gist: Thousands of Richmond County voters have cast early ballots ahead of the May 19 local elections. The ballot includes mayor, commission, judge, and tax questions.

Why It Matters: These are the races that decide how Augusta handles budgets, public safety, courts, and neighborhood services for the next few years.

What's Next: Advance voting runs through May 15. Election Day is Tuesday, May 19.

Read more here.

🔧 Low-Cost Repairs Get a Permanent Shop

The Gist: God's Grace Auto Repair, a mobile mechanic operation that fixes cars in customers' driveways, is moving into a permanent building. The group says the new space will help it expand low-cost repair work.

Why It Matters: In a car-dependent city, one repair bill can decide whether someone gets to work, school, appointments, or the grocery store.

What's Next: The permanent shop gives the group more room to serve drivers who need reliable transportation on tight budgets.

Read more here.

🏠 Youth Support Center Reopens After Helene

The Gist: The Living in Purpose Center has reopened after Hurricane Helene damage forced the youth support center to close. The center serves young people with mentoring, support, and a steady place to connect.

Why It Matters: A reopened youth space matters in a recovery year because families need normal routines and neighborhood resources back online.

What's Next: The center is back serving students as Helene recovery work continues around the region.
