🌳 Broad Street Tree Cuts Raise Maintenance Questions

The Gist: Augusta has removed dozens of trees along Broad Street while crews repair aging infrastructure, and the city says it plans to replant. WRDW reported roots had tangled with power meters and water lines, while construction exposed old brick, wood pipes, clay pipes and lead pipes from the 1960s. In 2022, the engineering director asked for $4 million in SPLOST 8 money for tree management and removal, but city leaders approved $1 million.

Why It Matters: Broad Street is the city's front door, and this work ties together shade, historic character, underground utilities and public safety. If the replacement trees are not maintained, the same root and rot problems could return after the new streetscape is finished.

What's Next: The city still has to replant along Broad Street, and Augusta's Tree Commission has five vacant seats to fill.

🏗️ Commission May Weigh Data Center Moratorium

The Gist: Augusta leaders could consider a moratorium on new data centers as neighbors keep pressing questions about the QTS project near Fort Gordon. WRDW reported the planned west Augusta campus is a $2 billion, six-building development that could eventually take up as much space as two Augusta Malls between Hayne's Station and Captain's Corner off Gordon Highway.

Why It Matters: This is one of Augusta's biggest development fights because the upside is tax base and construction work, while neighbors are asking about power use, water, noise, backup generators and whether residents were told enough before homes were built nearby.

What's Next: Commissioner Jordan Johnson said he would add the moratorium to the next full commission meeting. Augusta's 2026 schedule lists that meeting for Tuesday, June 2.

🏫 Firefighter Charged After School Inspection Probe

The Gist: Authorities say Augusta firefighter Tyrone Antonio Harris Jr. was booked after an investigation into unauthorized fire inspections at Richmond County schools. He faces five counts of impersonating a public officer or employee, with bond set at $9,000 on each charge. Warrants said Harris entered schools wearing an Augusta-Richmond County Fire Department uniform and carrying a clipboard.

Why It Matters: School access depends on staff being able to trust badges, uniforms and paperwork. The district said four schools sent letters to families about the incident, including Hephzibah High, Hephzibah Middle, Jamestown Elementary and the Alternative Center at Morgan Road.

Read more here.

🧹 Augusta Targets Illegal Dumping Ordinance Fixes

The Gist: City Administrator Tameka Allen told Augusta commissioners that officials toured some of the worst illegal dumping sites and found areas needing immediate attention. Multiple departments are now involved, including the Marshal's Office, Code Enforcement, Environmental Services, the Sheriff's Office and the Clean City Initiative. Officials also said many ordinances on illegal dumping, vacant lots and blight are more than a decade old.

Why It Matters: Blight enforcement is a basic city-services test: residents notice dumping fast, but outdated rules can slow cleanup and make responsibility murky across departments. The same discussion noted code enforcement and the Marshal's Office removed 2,042 illegal roadside signs in 2024, 1,900 in 2025 and 332 so far in 2026.

What's Next: Commissioners are expected to bring back updated recommendations and ordinance changes within 60 days.

Read more here.

🏒 Augusta Lynx Name Returns for New ECHL Team

The Gist: Augusta's new ECHL team revealed the Augusta Lynx name and logo Tuesday night at the Convention Center at Augusta Marriott. The league said the identity came from public naming input, with Tim Tebow and David Hodges owning the team and Zawyer Sports & Entertainment running operations.

Why It Matters: The familiar Lynx name gives Augusta a hockey identity before the new arena opens, and a 36-home-game season would bring steady downtown traffic.

What's Next: The team is set to begin play in the 2027-28 season at the 10,500-seat Augusta Arena.
