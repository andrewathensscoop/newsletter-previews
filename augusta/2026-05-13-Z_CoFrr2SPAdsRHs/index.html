🏊 Aquatics Center Closure Raises Fairness Questions

The Augusta Aquatics Center has been closed to public members for nearly a month over what city officials describe as a safety system issue, while a paying group has continued using the pool.

Why it matters: Residents are still paying for access they cannot use, and the exception turns a facilities problem into a trust problem.

What's next: City officials need to explain the repair timeline and how member access will be handled.

Read more here.

🏥 Eisenhower Downgrade Plan Draws More Questions

The Defense Health Agency has confirmed its intent to transition Eisenhower Army Medical Center toward an ambulatory care center model after months of local questions over what Fort Gordon families and retirees could lose.

Why it matters: This affects a huge military and retiree patient base that depends on the hospital for more than routine appointments.

What's next: Watch for congressional pressure and clearer timelines as local leaders press DHA for specifics.

Read more here.

🗳️ Early Voting Turns Out Thousands

Thousands of Richmond County voters have already cast early ballots ahead of local elections, putting the county among Georgia's stronger early-vote performers so far.

Why it matters: Mayor, commission, judge, and tax questions can shape the next few years of local government, even when turnout is usually lighter than a presidential cycle.

What's next: Election Day is May 19. Check your precinct and ballot before you go.

Read more here.

🔧 Low-Cost Auto Repair Gets a Permanent Home

God's Grace Auto Repair, a mobile mechanic operation that fixes cars in customers' driveways, is moving into a permanent building so it can keep expanding low-cost repairs.

Why it matters: In a car-dependent city, a repair bill can decide whether someone keeps a job, makes appointments, or gets kids to school.

What's next: The new building gives the group more room to serve clients who need reliable transportation on tight budgets.

Read more here.

🏠 Youth Support Center Reopens After Helene Damage

The Living in Purpose Center has reopened after Hurricane Helene damage forced the youth support center to close.

Why it matters: The center gives young people a stable place for support, mentoring, and community connection after a long disruption.

What's next: The reopening puts another neighborhood resource back online as recovery work continues across the area.
