Historic Deli Lunch on 6th Street

Located at 226 6th Street, Hildebrandt's has been part of Augusta since 1879. The family-owned German-inspired deli keeps lunch simple with stacked sandwiches, a famous Reuben, and German potato salad.

The room feels more like an old downtown store than a polished lunch spot. Grab a sandwich, browse the shop shelves, and settle into one of Augusta's longest-running local traditions.

🏗️ Augusta Opens Data Center Ordinance Review

The Gist: Augusta leaders are holding public meetings on a draft data center ordinance that has not yet been approved. Planning and Development Director Adleasia Cameron said the review is meant to update local rules around responsible growth, with meetings scheduled for July 6, July 14 and July 21 at the Municipal Building on Telfair Street.

Why It Matters: The debate is tied directly to concerns from Haynes Station residents near Gordon Highway, where groundwork began on a QTS data center site that neighbors say they did not fully understand when homes were built nearby. The new ordinance could shape how future projects deal with noise, water, power demand and public notice.

What's Next: The draft is scheduled for the Public Services Committee at 1 p.m. on July 14 and the Augusta Commission at 2 p.m. on July 21 in Room 291 of the Municipal Building, 535 Telfair Street. Residents can also review the draft and submit comments online through Augusta government.

🏥 Senators Push Back On Eisenhower Downgrade

The Gist: U.S. Sens. Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock are asking the Defense Health Agency to halt plans to downgrade Eisenhower Army Medical Center at Fort Gordon and rescind its congressional notification. The plan would close inpatient services except for the Residential Treatment Facility and convert the emergency room to urgent care.

Why It Matters: This is a major health care access issue for service members, military families, veterans and Augusta-area hospitals that could absorb more demand. The senators said referrals from the Augusta VA to Eisenhower rose from 179 patients in 2024 to 360 in 2025, with 110 more referrals already logged as of May 1 this year.

What's Next: Ossoff and Warnock asked the Defense Health Agency to answer their questions by July 17, 2026. WRDW said it has also asked DHA for a response.

🎓 AU And Fort Gordon Expand Degree Credit Path

The Gist: Augusta University President Russell T. Keen and Maj. Gen. Ryan M. Janovic, commanding general of the U.S. Army Cyber Center of Excellence, were set to sign a new memorandum at the Georgia Cyber Center on July 7. The agreement updates pathways for service members to receive degree credit for military training through AU and AU Online.

Why It Matters: Augusta's cyber economy runs through the university, the Georgia Cyber Center and Fort Gordon. The updated agreement covers graduate programs in cybersecurity management and technology, intelligence and security studies, technical intelligence analysis, and intelligence, defense and cybersecurity policy.

What's Next: The signing was scheduled for 11 a.m. July 7 at the Shaffer MacCartney Building at the Georgia Cyber Center. Service members interested in the programs should watch Augusta University and Fort Gordon channels for enrollment details.

🏀 Peach Jam Marks 30 Years In North Augusta

The Gist: North Augusta leaders have proclaimed July 2026 as the month celebrating the 30th anniversary of Nike Peach Jam at Riverview Park Activity Center. Destination Augusta and the Augusta Sports Council estimate more than 20,000 visitors and more than $11 million in economic impact this year.

Why It Matters: Peach Jam is one of the area's biggest sports tourism engines, bringing elite high school basketball teams, families, scouts and national attention to the Augusta market. WRDW reported the tournament helps support nearly 30,000 leisure and hospitality jobs across the area.

What's Next: The 2026 Peach Jam runs Wednesday, July 15 through Sunday, July 19 at Riverview Park Activity Center.

🎒 Back-To-School Giveaways Start This Weekend

The Gist: Local organizations are lining up school supply drives for the 2026-2027 school year. The list includes Hawk Law Group's Bookbag Bash at Bell Auditorium from 9 a.m. to noon on July 11, Victory Baptist Church at Summerfield Park from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. the same day, and a Risen Church giveaway at 670 E. Robinson Avenue in Grovetown.

Why It Matters: The first week of school gets expensive fast, especially for families buying supplies, clothes and haircuts at the same time. These events spread help across Augusta, Grovetown, Aiken County, Thomson, Harlem and other nearby communities.

What's Next: The first events are July 11, with more giveaways scheduled July 18, July 19, July 23, July 25 and July 31. Many are while supplies last, so families should check the event details before going.
