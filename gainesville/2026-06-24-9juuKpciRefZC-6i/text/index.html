🎓 Bell Named UF Interim President

🎓 Bell Named UF Interim President

The Gist: UF's Board of Trustees voted unanimously June 22 to appoint Stuart Bell as interim president after the Florida Board of Governors indefinitely delayed his confirmation vote. Bell had been scheduled for final consideration June 24, but Board of Governors Chair Alan Levine cited concerns that UF policies concentrate too much authority in individual trustees or board chairs.

Why It Matters: UF's leadership churn affects Gainesville's largest institution, including campus hiring, housing projects, hospital and research strategy, and the university's relationship with the city.

What's Next: Bell's interim appointment begins July 1 and runs through June 30, 2027 unless the Board of Governors confirms him as permanent president before then.

🏗️ Alachua Advances Walmart Plan

🏗️ Alachua Advances Walmart Plan

The Gist: Alachua City Commissioners voted to approve a comprehensive-plan change for a possible Walmart along U.S. 441 after dozens of residents packed City Hall on June 22. The hearing also covered a first reading and quasi-judicial zoning discussion for the 33-acre commercial development, with residents split over traffic, growth, and aquifer impacts.

Why It Matters: The project could reshape one of Alachua's busiest growth corridors and puts retail expansion, road capacity, and water-table concerns in the same local fight.

What's Next: A separate zoning decision still has to come before commissioners before the project can move ahead.

⛳ Ironwood Bids Open Up

⛳ Ironwood Bids Open Up

The Gist: Gainesville commissioners voted 6-1 on June 18 to open a 30-day window for proposals to purchase, lease, or partner on Ironwood Golf Course. HangTime Golf, owned by UF alumni Adam Rappaport and David Pearson, offered $1.1 million to buy the course, $600,000 beyond its remaining debt, and said it would invest $2.4 million in first-year improvements.

Why It Matters: Ironwood has been city-owned since 1992, needs capital investment, and is running about $320,000 over its annual expense budget, so the decision could change a major East Gainesville public asset.

What's Next: After the 30-day proposal window, commissioners can pick a proposal, pursue a lease or partnership, or reject all offers and keep city ownership.

🚰 Newberry Wastewater Plant Breaks Ground

🚰 Newberry Wastewater Plant Breaks Ground

The Gist: Newberry held a June 18 groundbreaking for an $80 million regional wastewater treatment plant at 26204 SW 18th Road. The plant will have 1.2 million gallons of capacity, with Archer taking 15% through a $12 million share covered by grants, and Newberry Mayor Tim Marden said most facility costs will be paid through developer impact and permit fees plus state grants.

Why It Matters: The project is a major west-county growth lever, giving Newberry and Archer sewer capacity for development while moving more Archer properties away from septic systems.

What's Next: Crews expect the plant to be completed in 2.5 to 3 years, with modular structures planned for future expansions.

🧱 Streatery Bricks Start Downtown

🧱 Streatery Bricks Start Downtown

The Gist: Brick paver installation is underway on Gainesville's $4.5 million Streatery project, and the first phase on SW Second Street is expected to reopen to traffic by the end of June. Crews have finished underground stormwater work, asphalt paving, and the concrete base for the brick pavers, while work continues on SW First Avenue between SW Second Street and SW First Street.

Why It Matters: The project is meant to turn SW First Avenue between South Main Street and SW Second Street into a curbless downtown corridor for outdoor dining, festivals, markets, and other public events.

What's Next: Businesses remain open during construction, SW Second Street is scheduled to reopen by the end of June, and full Streatery construction is expected to finish in October.
