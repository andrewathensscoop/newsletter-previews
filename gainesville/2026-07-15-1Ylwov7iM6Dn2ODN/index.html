🥖 House-made bread and local produce at South Main Station

Inside historic South Main Station across from Depot Park, Bingo Deli & Pub is chef Will Thomas's neighborhood restaurant and bar, built around fresh local produce and food made in-house.

The kitchen makes its own pasta, bread, sauces, and pickles, while the bar pours natural wines and fresh-ingredient cocktails. Gainesville artists rotate through the walls, and the restaurant has appeared on Food Network's Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives.

🏗️ Alachua's Farmlands vote draws legal challenge

The Gist: The Alachua City Commission voted 3-2 on June 22 to approve land-use changes for 46 acres of the Farmlands development along U.S. 441. Two outside attorneys say the city's comprehensive plan requires a second public hearing before officials can proceed with rezoning for the broader 200-plus-acre project, which includes 203 homes and planned commercial space.

Why It Matters: The dispute could affect a major development, a reported Walmart site and whether the city's approval survives a legal challenge.

What's Next: The rezoning continuation is scheduled first on the agenda at the commission's noon meeting July 27.

Read more here.

💸 Alachua County scam losses could near $9 million

The Gist: The Alachua County Sheriff's Office expects residents to lose nearly $9 million to scams in 2026, up from more than $6.8 million stolen last year. Officials said fraud losses now exceed local losses from theft, burglary, criminal mischief and vehicle break-ins combined, with AI making scams harder to spot.

Why It Matters: Older residents are being targeted heavily, and the sheriff says current budget allocations do not match the size of the problem.

What's Next: Residents who suspect a scam can call law enforcement before sending money, and Elder Options can connect older victims with advocates.

Read more here.

🥪 Free meals continue at 23 Alachua County schools

The Gist: All students at 23 Alachua County schools will receive free breakfast and lunch in 2026-27 through the federal Community Eligibility Provision. Families at the district's other 27 schools must apply for free or reduced-price meals, which cost 30 cents for breakfast and 40 cents for lunch when approved.

Why It Matters: The school list determines which families receive meals automatically and which need to complete paperwork before the new year.

What's Next: Applications are available online through Your Choice Fresh, with paper copies available at affected schools.

Read more here.

🌿 69-acre preserve opens near Waldo

The Gist: Little Lake Santa Fe Preserve is now open at 20201 NW 132nd Ave. near Waldo. The 69-acre site has a mile-long trail for hiking, bicycling and horseback riding, plus more than 800 feet of protected shoreline within the Santa Fe River headwaters and Florida Wildlife Corridor.

Why It Matters: The state-owned, county-managed preserve protects wetlands and water resources while adding a new public recreation spot in northeast Alachua County.

What's Next: The preserve is open daily from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. through September, then from 7 a.m. to 6 p.m. October through March.

Read more here.

🏬 Celebration Pointe plan depends on $50 million

The Gist: A third bankruptcy plan for three Celebration Pointe entities depends on primary investor Patricia Shively contributing more than $50 million over time, including at least $8.5 million when the plan takes effect. The Chapter 11 case has lasted more than 26 months and involves nine major creditors, other claims and about $780,000 in unpaid taxes.

Why It Matters: A failed agreement could trigger an uncoordinated Chapter 7 liquidation at a major retail, residential and events hub tied to county infrastructure.

What's Next: A separate lawsuit involving Shively has a final mediation deadline of Nov. 30.
