Mildred's Big City Food

🎓 Bell Contract Heads to State Vote

🎓 Bell Contract Heads to State Vote

The Gist: The Florida Board of Governors is scheduled to vote Wednesday on a five-year employment agreement that would make Stuart Bell UF's 14th president. The proposed contract starts July 1, runs through June 30, 2031, and includes a $2 million annual base salary, up to 3% annual merit increases, performance incentives, retention payments, and a guaranteed faculty appointment if Bell completes the term or is removed without cause.

Why It Matters: UF is Gainesville's biggest institution, so the president shapes campus hiring, research growth, hospital strategy, construction, athletics, housing pressure, and the university's relationship with city and county leaders.

What's Next: The Board of Governors vote is scheduled for Wednesday, July 1, after a June 24 delay tied to broader governance concerns.

🏥 Shands Nurses Ratify $18.3M Deal

🏥 Shands Nurses Ratify $18.3M Deal

The Gist: The Shands Nurses Union, OPEIU Local 713 and Florida Nurses Association, ratified a new three-year contract with UF Health Shands Hospital after five days of bargaining. The agreement covers 2,952 bedside registered nurses, includes $18.3 million in wage and compensation improvements, adds $3.3 million to keep health insurance premiums flat in 2027, and gives hourly raises ranging from $1 to $4.

Why It Matters: Shands is one of Gainesville's central employers and health care anchors, so nurse staffing, wages, and workplace voice affect both local families and patient care across the region.

What's Next: The contract includes a wage reopener clause that allows new wage negotiations to start in spring 2027.

🏛️ Thelma Boltin Site Planning Starts

🏛️ Thelma Boltin Site Planning Starts

The Gist: Gainesville hosted the first of three community workshops Tuesday on the former Thelma A. Boltin Center site. Commissioners voted last September to demolish the building and reimagine the property as a Sweetwater Greenway Loop trailhead, with outdoor community space, stormwater improvements, a community garden, and features tied to the site's World War II history and Thelma Boltin's legacy.

Why It Matters: The city has about $2 million in Wild Spaces & Public Places money budgeted for the site, but trail connection funding is still uncertain and residents are still questioning what was lost during demolition.

What's Next: The planning process is expected to take several months, with two more workshops planned and online survey input available through the city's Wild Spaces and Public Places website.

🏗️ The Verve Density Clears Plan Board

🏗️ The Verve Density Clears Plan Board

The Gist: The Gainesville City Plan Board voted 4-2 Thursday to recommend higher density for The Verve, a planned 12-story building at 1026 SW Second Avenue near UF and the Innovation District. The amendment would add 23 units and 125 bedrooms, bringing the project to 227 units and 682 bedrooms, with 24 affordable units at 50% to 80% of area median income and parking rising from 90 to 100 spaces.

Why It Matters: The project sits beside the University Heights Historic District and would reshape a tight corridor near University Avenue, forcing Gainesville to balance student housing demand, scale, parking, and on-site affordable housing.

What's Next: The Gainesville City Commission still has to decide whether to approve the density amendment and whether the 24 affordable units must stay on site instead of being replaced by a cash payment.

🚓 High Springs Chief Draws Support

🚓 High Springs Chief Draws Support

The Gist: Hundreds of residents are speaking out after High Springs City Manager Jeremy Marshall placed Police Chief Antoine Sheppard on indefinite paid administrative leave June 23 during an undisclosed investigation into complaints. Sheppard has served as chief since 2019, recently marked 25 years with the department, and the city says it cannot comment while the investigation is active.

Why It Matters: High Springs already spent spring budget talks weighing cuts to police and fire, so the chief's leave has turned into a larger fight over public safety leadership, city transparency, and trust in local government.

What's Next: Supporters plan a 5:30 p.m. prayer meeting July 7 at City Hall before attending the 6:30 p.m. High Springs City Commission meeting.
