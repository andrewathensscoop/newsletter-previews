🎓 Bell Confirmed as UF President

🎓 Bell Confirmed as UF President

The Gist: The Florida Board of Governors confirmed Stuart Bell as UF's 14th president at a July 1 special meeting, with only Governor Aubrey Edge voting no. Bell, the former University of Alabama president, immediately assumed the role after unanimous approval from UF trustees in June and will earn a $2 million base salary under an agreement running through June 30, 2031.

Why It Matters: UF is Gainesville's largest institutional anchor, so its president shapes campus construction, research funding, hospital growth, athletics, jobs, and the university's relationship with the city after several years of leadership turnover.

🏫 ACPS Holds District B Grade

🏫 ACPS Holds District B Grade

The Gist: Alachua County Public Schools maintained a district B for 2025-26 after Florida raised grading thresholds. Twenty-one traditional public schools earned A or B grades, seven traditional schools held A grades, Rawlings Elementary fell from C to F, and district English Language Arts proficiency rose from 55% in 2025 to 59% in 2026.

Why It Matters: School grades influence family decisions, housing demand, state oversight, and the district's Future Ready planning, especially with Foster already closed and Rawlings, Lake Forest, Metcalfe and Williams receiving D or F grades.

🏥 Shands Nurses Ratify 3-Year Deal

🏥 Shands Nurses Ratify 3-Year Deal

The Gist: The Shands Nurses Union, OPEIU Local 713 and Florida Nurses Association ratified a new three-year contract with UF Health Shands Hospital covering 2,952 bedside registered nurses. The deal includes $18.3 million in wage and compensation improvements, $3.3 million to keep 2027 health insurance premiums flat, $1 to $4 hourly raises, and merit increases averaging 3.8%.

Why It Matters: UF Health Shands is one of Gainesville's biggest employers and the region's flagship hospital, so nurse pay, retention and workplace voice affect both local jobs and patient care capacity.

What's Next: The contract includes a wage re-opener, allowing new wage negotiations to start in spring 2027.

🏟️ Boys & Girls Club Sale Draws Pushback

🏟️ Boys & Girls Club Sale Draws Pushback

The Gist: The Boys & Girls Clubs of Northeast Florida wants to sell the Northwest Gainesville club for a rumored $2.5 million, six years after receiving the 8.5-acre site and 19,500-square-foot building. Local leaders say youth sports programs have been cut, 300 to 400 young athletes are in limbo, and the 2020 acquisition agreement restricts property sales for 10 years.

Why It Matters: The site has been a youth sports and after-school hub for decades, and Cox recently invested $25,000 in an Innovation Lab there, so losing local control would hit families, neighborhood programs and future recreation options in northwest Gainesville.

What's Next: Doug Johnson, who has a right of first refusal on the property, said legal action is likely if Jacksonville leadership continues pursuing the sale.

🏗️ Alachua Advances Farmlands Plan

🏗️ Alachua Advances Farmlands Plan

The Gist: The Alachua City Commission voted 3-2 on June 22 to approve land-use changes for 46 acres of the Farmlands Commercial project along U.S. 441 near NW 188th Street. The broader plan covers more than 200 acres and includes over 200 detached homes, a 423,400-square-foot shopping center, a 345,000-square-foot main shopping center, restaurants, a gas station and an auto center.

Why It Matters: Farmlands could reshape one of western Alachua County's fastest-growing corridors, with residents split over traffic, groundwater, sinkhole risk, tax revenue and the long-running possibility of a Walmart in Alachua.

What's Next: Commissioners deferred the 182-acre planned-development rezoning hearing to July 27.
