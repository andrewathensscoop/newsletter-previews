🏛️ GRU Control Bill Becomes Law

🏛️ GRU Control Bill Becomes Law

The Gist: Gov. Ron DeSantis signed House Bill 1451 on June 12, adding language that appears to preempt changes to the Gainesville Regional Utilities Authority unless the Florida Legislature acts. The House passed the bill 85-26 and the Senate passed it 30-6, and GRU is the only authority affected by the amendment.

Why It Matters: Gainesville voters have twice backed dissolving the governor-appointed authority, so the new law raises the stakes for home rule, utility bills, and ongoing court fights over who controls GRU.

What's Next: Three First District Court of Appeal cases tied to the GRU Authority remain active, including one that had oral arguments in February.

🗳️ Gainesville Ballot Takes Shape

🗳️ Gainesville Ballot Takes Shape

The Gist: The qualifying period closed June 12 with 10 candidates qualified for Gainesville's 2026 city races, though District 4 Commissioner Bryan Eastman is unopposed. Mayor Harvey Ward faces Evan Moon, District 2 has Nathan Crabbe, Nelida Pellot and Wyatt Zintiz, and District 3 has incumbent Casey Willits against DeJeon Cain and James Gardner.

Why It Matters: The mayor and commission seats will shape housing, roads, GRU oversight, garbage and stormwater billing, and the city budget for the next four years.

What's Next: Election Day is Tuesday, Aug. 18. The voter registration deadline is July 20, and early voting runs Aug. 7-15 from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.

🏟️ School Projects Hit $236M

🏟️ School Projects Hit $236M

The Gist: ACPS Chief Financial Officer Michael Reneke laid out seven capital projects totaling more than $236 million, including $30.5 million for Citizens Field, $65 million for Mebane work, about $32 million at Oak View, roughly $5.5 million in middle school gym repairs, a little more than $1.7 million for track rubberization, and $5.4 million at Newberry High.

Why It Matters: These projects are tied to right-sizing, school closures, Newberry Elementary's charter conversion, and whether the district can secure a long-term stadium plan for high school football and community events.

What's Next: The board is scheduled to revisit Citizens Field agreements at a 5:30 p.m. special meeting June 16, then bring COPS financing documents for a July 30 vote.

🎓 UF Trustees Pick Bell

🎓 UF Trustees Pick Bell

The Gist: The UF Board of Trustees voted unanimously June 10 to select Stuart R. Bell, former University of Alabama president, as UF's 14th president. Bell would follow Ben Sasse's 2024 resignation, Santa Ono's failed 10-6 Board of Governors vote, interim Kent Fuchs, and interim President Donald Landry.

Why It Matters: UF's president affects the region's biggest employer, research engine, hospital ecosystem, athletics, campus growth, and town-gown decisions that spill into Gainesville neighborhoods.

What's Next: The Florida Board of Governors is expected to vote on Bell's ratification at its June 25 meeting.

🚰 Golf Course, Pipeline Advance

🚰 Golf Course, Pipeline Advance

The Gist: The Alachua County Commission approved UF's 580-acre, 36-hole golf course on Parker Road and allowed Archer to build an eight-mile wastewater force main to Newberry's planned regional facility. The golf project can use up to 2 million gallons of irrigation water per day from lakes, stormwater and reuse sources, while the Archer line has a $5.8 million budget and 175,000 gallons per day of capacity.

Why It Matters: The votes put water use, western-county growth, regional sewer service, and UF land development in the same conversation for Archer, Newberry, and nearby unincorporated residents.

What's Next: The golf course now moves to a preliminary development plan expected back before the County Commission in the fall. Archer's grants for the pump station and wastewater line expire in October, so the city is accelerating design approvals.
