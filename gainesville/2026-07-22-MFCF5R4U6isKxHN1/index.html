🍔 McMillan family burgers on NW 10th since 1987

At 129 NW 10th Avenue, Mac's Drive-Thru is the McMillan family's old-school burger window, opened by Glen McMillan and his sons Jon and Ken in 1987.

Burgers are cooked to order Mac's Way or All-the-Way, with fries, fish sandwiches, and hot ham and cheese rounding out the short menu. The no-frills setup and steady lunch line are part of the Gainesville ritual.

💰 Gainesville advances $7.8 million fire-fee increase

The Gist: The Gainesville City Commission kept the maximum property tax rate at 6.7297 mills while advancing a plan that could raise fire assessments by about $7.8 million. The proposal also cuts $1.4 million from departments and uses $1.2 million from the fleet fund balance to help close a $10.4 million budget gap.

Why It Matters: Property owners could pay a higher fire fee even without a millage-rate increase, and larger apartment buildings would carry more of the assessment.

What's Next: Commissioners requested another budget meeting for Aug. 6, with final tax and fire-assessment votes due in September.

Read more here.

🛣️ County road backlog pushes CR 337 work to 2030

The Gist: Residents say deteriorating County Road 337 has gone without meaningful attention for decades, and work once expected in 2027 has shifted to 2030. Alachua County says 84 transportation projects totaling about $121 million were completed, under construction or in planning as of May.

Why It Matters: The county is working through decades of deferred maintenance while inflation stretches its 10-year, $250 million transportation program.

What's Next: Residents can track completed, active and planned work on the county's Transportation Capital Improvement Program map.

Read more here.

🚒 Newberry buys 8.83 acres for fire training hub

The Gist: Newberry commissioners unanimously approved a $600,000 purchase of 8.83 acres west of Champions Park for an Environmental Park expansion and regional fire-training facility. The city has $750,000 in state funding for the burn box but expects to borrow about $250,000 toward its estimated $1 million construction cost.

Why It Matters: Training locally could reduce the roughly $150,000 Newberry spends each year sending firefighters to Lofton and keep more crews available for calls.

What's Next: Burn-box construction is scheduled to begin during the 2026-27 fiscal year.

Read more here.

🎨 Santa Fe lands $2.5 million for arts overhaul

The Gist: Santa Fe College received a $2.5 million state appropriation to renovate the M Building on its Northwest Campus. Built in 1976, the building serves about 2,500 visual arts students each year and will gain a fabrication lab plus upgraded 3D, sculpture and woodworking spaces.

Why It Matters: The project updates aging classrooms and labs while expanding hands-on training for visual arts, graphic design and digital media students.

What's Next: Trustees are expected to consider Phase 1 this fall, with that work scheduled for completion by summer 2027.

Read more here.

💧 Water limits extended through Dec. 31

The Gist: The Suwannee River Water Management District extended its Modified Phase II Water Shortage Order through Dec. 31, including unincorporated Alachua County. The region ended June more than 18 inches below normal rainfall, with groundwater around the 12th percentile.

Why It Matters: Established lawns remain limited to one irrigation day per week, and cosmetic pressure washing stays prohibited as drought conditions persist.

What's Next: Odd-numbered homes may irrigate Saturdays, even-numbered homes Sundays and nonresidential properties Tuesdays, all outside 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.
