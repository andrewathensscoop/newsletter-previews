🍽️ The Floridian settles into Old Beach Road

Located at 485 Old Beach Road, The Floridian is back open in its new beach-side home after years as a St. Augustine standby. Genie and Jeff McNally started it in 2010, and the kitchen still leans on fresh, farm-to-table cooking for lunch, happy hour, and dinner.

The best detail is the supplier list: Wainwright Dairy, Jax Bread Co., Ancient City Micro Greens, Beacon Fisheries, The Datil Farm, and more. It reads like a restaurant still built around nearby growers, bakers, and fish houses, even after the move.

🏫 St. Johns schools rank No. 1 in Florida

The Gist: St. Johns County School District says it earned Florida's No. 1 district ranking for 2026, with an A district grade and 910 accountability points. The district posted the announcement July 1 under the banner Shine Bright with the BEST.

Why It Matters: For families in St. Augustine and across St. Johns County, the ranking reinforces one of the county's biggest quality-of-life and housing-market drivers: high-performing public schools.

What's Next: The School Board's next regular meeting is July 14 at 9 a.m. at the SJCSD Administrative Building, 40 Orange Street in St. Augustine. A special meeting on advertising the superintendent's tentative budget is set for July 23 at 4:30 p.m.

Read more here.

💧 Ponte Vedra water plant gets $1.624M upgrade

The Gist: St. Johns County Utility Department completed a $1,624,350 capital improvement project at the Plantation Water Treatment Plant in Ponte Vedra Beach. The work replaced a more than 40-year-old metal aerator structure with reinforced concrete and a corrosion-resistant aluminum aerator while the plant stayed operational.

Why It Matters: The plant is a key part of Ponte Vedra Beach's drinking water system, and the county says the upgrade should reduce future maintenance while improving resilience in a coastal environment.

Read more here.

🏖️ Free beach shuttle runs through July 6

The Gist: St. Johns County Beach Services is offering a free Fourth of July weekend shuttle from Cornerstone Park at 1046 A1A North to Mickler's Landing Beachfront Park. The shuttle runs July 4 through July 6 from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.

Why It Matters: The county is trying to take pressure off one of the busiest beach weekends of the year, with lifeguards on duty from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. and sheriff's deputies, St. Augustine Beach police, and Marine Rescue patrolling county beaches.

What's Next: The shuttle continues Sunday, July 5 and Monday, July 6 from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Beach driving is open from 8 a.m. to 7:30 p.m., conditions permitting.

Read more here.

💼 Tourism impact hits $3.8B in St. Johns County

The Gist: St. Johns County says tourism provides $3.8 billion in economic impact, supports 32,400 jobs, and generates $53 million in local sales and property tax revenue. County Commission Chair Clay Murphy spoke at the 2026 State of the Tourism Industry Celebration at Embassy Suites by Hilton St. Augustine Beach Oceanfront Resort.

Why It Matters: Tourism is still one of the county's central economic engines, and the tax revenue tied to visitors helps fund services that residents use year-round.

Read more here.

🐾 Two charged after 23 dogs dumped in St. Augustine

The Gist: News4JAX reports that Austin Moore, 32, and Anthony Dunham, 23, face animal cruelty charges after investigators said 23 dogs were abandoned near US-1 and County Road 204 in St. Augustine. The dogs were found May 4, three died, and the remaining 20 were taken to local animal hospitals.

Why It Matters: The case pulled in both the St. Johns County Sheriff's Office and Flagler County Sheriff's Office, and it highlights the local burden that large animal cruelty cases place on law enforcement, shelters, and veterinary care.
