🗳️ Fake Voter Guide Case Hits County Offices

🗳️ Fake Voter Guide Case Hits County Offices

The Gist: News4JAX reports that five people were charged in connection with an alleged fake Republican voter guide distributed during the 2024 St. Johns County primary. The defendants include Brianna Jordan, County Commissioner Sarah Arnold, St. Augustine Beach Commissioner and former mayor Dylan Rumrell, County Commissioner Christian Whitehurst, and Jamie Johnson.

Why It Matters: The case lands in the middle of county government because Arnold and Whitehurst are sitting commissioners, while Rumrell serves on the St. Augustine Beach commission. Prosecutors allege the guide falsely presented itself as an official St. Johns GOP publication and lacked required authorization and disclosures.

What's Next: Arrest warrants were received by the St. Johns County Sheriff's Office on July 6. Jordan faces an additional third-degree felony evidence-tampering count with a $12,000 total bond, while the other listed charges are misdemeanors.

🚧 Rail Crossings Close On West King And Kinlaw

🚧 Rail Crossings Close On West King And Kinlaw

The Gist: St. Johns County says railroad crossing maintenance will close the W. King Street and Kinlaw Road crossings to all traffic, including vehicles, bicycles, and pedestrians. The W. King Street closure began July 7 at 7 a.m., and the Kinlaw Road closure begins July 10 at 7 a.m.

Why It Matters: Both crossings sit in the St. Augustine traffic network, so the work will affect West Augustine drivers, downtown access, school and neighborhood trips, and anyone moving between US-1, King Street, and the south side of the city.

What's Next: Each closure is expected to last about four days. Drivers should use posted detours, plan extra travel time, and check the county's detailed detour maps before heading through the area.

💧 County's Biggest Capital Project Comes Online

💧 County's Biggest Capital Project Comes Online

The Gist: St. Johns County says the $191.8 million State Road 207 Water Reclamation Facility came online May 11 at 3055 SR 207 in Elkton. The project includes the treatment facility, multiple pump stations, and about 15 miles of wastewater and reclaimed water pipelines.

Why It Matters: The facility is the largest capital improvement project in county history and is built to conserve drinking water by producing reclaimed water for irrigation. The county says it should prevent more than one million pounds of nutrient pollutants from entering the Matanzas River over the next 20 years.

What's Next: St. Johns County plans to host an official ribbon-cutting ceremony in late summer. The facility starts with 3.25 million gallons per day of treatment capacity and can expand to 6.5 million gallons per day.

🏫 St. Johns Schools Rank No. 1 In Florida

🏫 St. Johns Schools Rank No. 1 In Florida

The Gist: St. Johns County School District announced July 1 that it earned Florida's No. 1 district ranking, an A district grade, and 910 accountability points. The district credited students, teachers, staff, families, and the wider community for another top-rated year.

Why It Matters: Public schools are one of St. Johns County's biggest quality-of-life drivers, and the ranking matters for families choosing where to live, employers recruiting workers, and neighborhoods tied to fast school growth.

What's Next: The School Board's next regular meeting is July 14 at 9 a.m. at the SJCSD Administrative Building, 40 Orange Street in St. Augustine. A special meeting on advertising the superintendent's tentative budget is set for July 23 at 4:30 p.m.

🏘️ Neighborhood Grant Window Opens In St. Augustine

🏘️ Neighborhood Grant Window Opens In St. Augustine

The Gist: The City of St. Augustine opened its Neighborhood Services Neighborhood Grant Program application period on July 1. The program offers up to $3,000 for formally and informally organized nonprofit groups working on small-scale neighborhood improvement, enrichment, enhancement projects, or events.

Why It Matters: This is one of the simpler ways neighborhood groups can get city backing for block-level projects without waiting on a major capital plan. The city says the money also comes with staff technical assistance to help groups plan and carry out the work.

What's Next: Applications close Wednesday, July 15, at 5 p.m. They can be submitted by email, mail, or in person at the Community Services Office in Lobby B on the fourth floor of City Hall, 75 King Street.
