☣️ Toxic Gas Forces Chewy Evacuation

☣️ Toxic Gas Forces Chewy Evacuation

The Gist: Ocala Fire Rescue evacuated Chewy's 600,000-square-foot fulfillment center on Northwest 35th Avenue Road around 8 a.m. Tuesday after a medic's carbon monoxide alarm sounded. Monitors detected a toxic, odorless gas, possibly linked to hydrogen off-gassing from forklift batteries. Six people were assessed, and one went to the hospital.

Why It Matters: The several-hour shutdown exposed a workplace safety risk at one of Ocala's large distribution facilities and required a full-building emergency response.

What's Next: Crews removed the forklifts and ventilated the building until gas concentrations fell and conditions were cleared.

🐊 Viral Gator Video Prompts FWC Investigation

🐊 Viral Gator Video Prompts FWC Investigation

The Gist: The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission is investigating a viral video showing a woman petting a wild alligator at Silver Springs State Park. Florida law prohibits feeding, enticing, touching, or capturing alligators unless someone is a licensed trapper or permitted hunter.

Why It Matters: Wildlife guide Josh Swander warned that viral encounters can habituate or endanger alligators, while the attention is already drawing visitors to seek sightings at one of Marion County's signature attractions.

What's Next: FWC's investigation is continuing, and the woman shown in the video could face charges.

Read more here.

⛈️ Storms Knock Out Power to Thousands

⛈️ Storms Knock Out Power to Thousands

The Gist: Severe thunderstorms toppled trees, damaged homes, and cut electricity to thousands of Ocala Electric Utility customers over the weekend. A large tree crashed onto a home near Northeast 17th Street while a daughter and grandchildren were inside, but no one was injured.

Why It Matters: The damage displaced at least one family and left neighborhoods facing cleanup, property questions, and the hazards of downed power lines.

What's Next: Ocala Electric Utility said most service had been restored and crews would continue working on remaining outages.

📡 Neighbors Build Off-Grid Emergency Network

📡 Neighbors Build Off-Grid Emergency Network

The Gist: Residents are expanding Marion Mesh, a long-range, low-power radio network that can pass emergency messages without internet or cellular service. Organizers want to place small solar-powered devices at churches and other community sites to broaden coverage across Marion County.

Why It Matters: A countywide backup network could keep neighbors connected when hurricanes or severe storms take conventional communications offline.

What's Next: The next Marion Mesh Meetup is scheduled for Sept. 5 and is open to newcomers.

Read more here.

🌳 County Weighs $195 Million Parks Plan

🌳 County Weighs $195 Million Parks Plan

The Gist: A Marion County proposal calls for about $128 million in repairs, renovations, and expansions at 54 existing parks and recreation sites, plus $67 million for seven potential new sites. Commissioners are reviewing how to prioritize nearly $195 million in projects with limited funding.

Why It Matters: The choices will shape recreation access, park conditions, and capital spending across communities throughout Marion County.

What's Next: County commissioners have another Parks and Recreation Department funding workshop scheduled for Aug. 5 at 2 p.m.
