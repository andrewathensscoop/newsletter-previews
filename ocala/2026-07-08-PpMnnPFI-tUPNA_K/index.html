Sliders and Weekend Music on SW Broadway

On SW Broadway Street downtown, The Black Sheep On Broadway is a woman-owned local bar and grill built around sliders, handhelds, wings, cold beer, and late-night hours.

The room is part of the draw, with live music on weekends, a customer-controlled jukebox, and plates built for sharing before a show, from wings and nachos to sliders with the house sauce.

🏫 MCPS Misses B Grade by One Point

The Gist: Marion County Public Schools said the district received a C for 2026 but missed a B by one point under the new grading scale. Traditional public schools posted 8 A grades, 14 B grades, 26 C grades, and no D or F grades, while 5,698 students earned at least one Level 5 on state testing.

Why It Matters: The headline grade is a setback, but the school-level results give parents a more specific picture of where campuses improved and where the district still has ground to make up.

Read more here.

🏛️ The Lodge Closed as Unsafe

The Gist: Ocala officials posted an unsafe structure notice at the downtown building that houses The Lodge, forcing it to stay closed until the issue is repaired, vacated, or demolished. Owner Johnny Sanchez told WCJB he believes the problem is a simple fix, while property records show the original First National Bank building dates to about 1886 with a western addition around 1912.

Why It Matters: The closure hits one of downtown's familiar nightlife spots and highlights the maintenance stakes for Ocala's older historic buildings.

What's Next: The Lodge cannot reopen until the building is cleared after repairs.

Read more here.

🐎 WEC Summer Series Draws 1,300 Horses

The Gist: The Summer Series at World Equestrian Center is in its fifth week, with around 1,300 horses competing in hunter and jumper events. WCJB reports more than $500,000 in prize money is on the line, and this week's competition is using six air-conditioned indoor arenas.

Why It Matters: WEC is one of Marion County's biggest tourism and equestrian economy engines, and a summer event of this size keeps riders, visitors, hotels, and restaurants moving even in July heat.

What's Next: The Summer Series continues this week at World Equestrian Center.

Read more here.

🎆 Belleview Hosts First Fireworks Show

The Gist: Belleview hosted its first Independence Day fireworks show at the Belleview Sportsplex for America's 250th birthday. WCJB reported thousands attended, with food trucks, vendors, face painting, family activities, and a 9:15 p.m. fireworks show.

Why It Matters: The event gave south Marion families a large local celebration without having to drive into Ocala, and ticketed activities helped support Belleview's school supply drive.

What's Next: Organizers said they hope the first fireworks celebration becomes an annual tradition.

Read more here.

⚡ Lightning Sparks Marion Oaks Attic Fire

The Gist: A lightning strike sparked an attic fire Tuesday at a single-family home in the 14900 block of SW 38th Circle in Marion Oaks. Marion County Fire Rescue arrived around 3 p.m., brought in crews from multiple stations, and had the fire under control in less than 30 minutes with no injuries reported.

Why It Matters: Afternoon storms are routine in Marion County, but this response shows how quickly a summer lightning strike can become a structure fire.
