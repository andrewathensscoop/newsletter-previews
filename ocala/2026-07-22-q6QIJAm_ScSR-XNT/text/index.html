🖥️ County Sets Data Center Rules Workshop

🖥️ County Sets Data Center Rules Workshop

The Gist: Marion County commissioners voted July 21 to hold a workshop on guidelines for potential data centers. Chair Carl Zalak III said no applications are pending and current rules limit the facilities to industrial land with a special use permit.

Why It Matters: Residents raised concerns about water, electricity, noise, wildlife and livestock before any developer reaches the application stage.

What's Next: The workshop is scheduled for 2 p.m. Sept. 1 at the McPherson Governmental Campus Auditorium, 601 SE 25th Ave.

📚 Silver Springs Shores Gets New Library

📚 Silver Springs Shores Gets New Library

The Gist: Marion County is opening a Silver Springs Shores Public Library at 527 Spring Lake Road with a children's reading area, computers, Wi-Fi and programs for all ages. Officials expect the branch to serve more than 15,000 local library cardholders.

Why It Matters: The branch brings a long-requested public resource directly into one of Marion County's largest residential communities.

What's Next: The grand opening begins at 10 a.m. July 23 with a ribbon cutting and tours. Regular hours will be 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. weekdays and 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Saturdays.

🩺 MTC Launches 12-Month RN Path

🩺 MTC Launches 12-Month RN Path

The Gist: Marion Technical College and HCA hospitals launched a 12-month diploma program that helps licensed practical nurses become registered nurses. MTC says it is the first institution in North Central Florida to offer the diploma, and 12 students are in the inaugural class.

Why It Matters: The shorter local pathway gives working nurses another route to advance while adding registered nurses to Marion County's health care workforce.

Read more here.

🏘️ Bellehaven Plans About 500 Homes

🏘️ Bellehaven Plans About 500 Homes

The Gist: Bellehaven, a four-phase residential project off Southeast Baseline Road, is planned for about 500 homes across nearly 220 acres. Belleview's population rose from roughly 5,500 in 2020 to more than 6,500 in 2025, and no homes have been built on the site yet.

Why It Matters: The project could help meet housing demand, but residents are pressing city leaders over added traffic, tree removal and the pace of Belleview's growth.

Read more here.

🔥 Weirsdale Factory Fire Collapses Building

🔥 Weirsdale Factory Fire Collapses Building

The Gist: Marion County Fire Rescue reached a factory in the 16600 block of South Highway 25 shortly after 4 a.m. July 19 and found about half the building engulfed. The structure collapsed and pulled down power lines, but crews controlled the fire shortly after 5 a.m. and reported no injuries.

Why It Matters: The collapse created hazards for neighboring homes and nearby woodlands, making the quick containment important for the Weirsdale area.
