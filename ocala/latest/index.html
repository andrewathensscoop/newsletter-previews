Ocala Barbecue With a Food-Truck Start

At 2611 SW 19th Avenue Road, Big Lee's Serious About BBQ is Rashad Jones' Ocala barbecue shop, a food-truck start that grew into a permanent restaurant and mobile-truck fleet.

The name goes back to Big Lee, Jones' wife's uncle Leon, whose Mississippi Delta smoker inspired the business. Build the table around brisket, burnt ends, ribs, pulled pork, and mac and cheese.

🗳️ Write-Ins Could Close Local Primaries

The Gist: Ocala Gazette reported two write-in filings in County Commission Districts 2 and 4, a move that could keep 127,131 independent and Democratic voters out of those GOP primaries. Mark Okus filed in District 2 and Leonard Michael Racioppi filed in District 4.

Why It Matters: Commission seats shape growth, roads, public safety spending and land-use decisions, so closed primaries would narrow local say.

What's Next: The primary is Aug. 18, with the general on Nov. 3.

Read more here.

🚔 52 Arrested in Child Predator Sting

The Gist: The Marion County Sheriff's Office said Operation Bad Habits, a weeklong undercover operation targeting online child exploitation, resulted in 52 arrests. WCJB reported detectives posed online as children ages 7 to 15, and suspects named by the sheriff's office included a Fessenden Elementary teacher, a briefly hired youth football coach and a 16-year-old high school student from Ocala.

Why It Matters: The case touches schools, youth sports and online safety at once, giving parents and local organizations a clear reason to revisit screening, supervision and reporting practices.

Read more here.

🚧 SW 66th Street Bridge Closing for a Year

The Gist: The SW 66th Street bridge over I-75 closes June 15 for FDOT's Moving I-75 Forward project. Marion County says the yearlong replacement will make the bridge higher and wider for drivers, pedestrians, bicyclists and mobility device users.

Why It Matters: This is a major route change for southwest Ocala, and officials noted more than one collision has occurred in the area.

What's Next: Traffic detours to SW 43rd Street Road. SW 38th Avenue also closes at SW 66th Street for about two weeks, with drivers routed via SW 80th Street and SW 49th Avenue.

Read more here.

♻️ Curbside Recycling Ends July 6

The Gist: Ocala ends curbside recycling July 6 and moves to drop-off-only service. City leaders said the switch avoids about $5 a month in new sanitation costs, with eight recycling sites including Mary Sue Rich Community Center, Ocala Regional Sportsplex, Polly Palmer Park and Jervey Gantt Park.

Why It Matters: The change alters weekly routines for city households and shifts more sorting and hauling to residents.

What's Next: Recycling carts become garbage carts July 6 unless residents request removal. Questions go to Ocala Sanitation at 352-351-6697.

Read more here.

🚑 Paramedicine Program Keeps Growing

The Gist: Marion County's community paramedicine program is expanding after serving more than 2,000 people. Ocala Fire Rescue began the work in 2017, launched the formal program in 2020 with AdventHealth Ocala and the Community Foundation, and now focuses on chronic illness, frequent emergency calls and opioid recovery.

Why It Matters: Preventive home visits can connect residents to care and recovery resources before trouble becomes an ambulance ride.

What's Next: Residents can use the referral form or call the 24-hour helpline at 352-266-4769.

Read more here.
