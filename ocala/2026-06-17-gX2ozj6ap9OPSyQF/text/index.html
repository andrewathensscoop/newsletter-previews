🏘️ 16,000-Home District Heads to Governor

🏘️ 16,000-Home District Heads to Governor

The Gist: HB 4079, the bill creating the Uplands Stewardship District west of On Top of the World, is on Gov. Ron DeSantis's desk after unanimous House and Senate votes. The district would be able to tax and provide infrastructure for up to 16,000 new homes.

Why It Matters: For west Ocala, that means the next wave of growth could arrive with its own financing district for roads, utilities and other infrastructure.

What's Next: The governor has until the end of June to act on the bill.

Read more here.

🐎 OBS June Sale Opens at $9M

🐎 OBS June Sale Opens at $9M

The Gist: Ocala Breeders' Sales opened its June Sale with 163 horses sold for more than $9 million in the first session. The session topper was Hip 168, a colt by Preakness winner Early Voting that sold for $410,000.

Why It Matters: The sale is a direct read on Ocala's horse economy, bringing buyers, consignors and spending into the local equine business base.

What's Next: The three-day sale continues at OBS through June 18.

🏫 South Marion High Adds 700 Seats

🏫 South Marion High Adds 700 Seats

The Gist: Marion County School Board members unanimously approved construction updates for South Marion High School, which opens in August. The district says finishing the third floor will add 700 student spaces to a campus expected to open at or beyond its 2,000-student capacity, with another $536,650 in design and service fees tied to the school's $165 million price tag.

Why It Matters: South Marion is being expanded before students arrive, which shows how fast enrollment pressure is building in the county's southwest growth corridor.

What's Next: The additional phase will not be finished in time for opening.

Read more here.

🚒 Firefighter Contract Adds $20K Raises

🚒 Firefighter Contract Adds $20K Raises

The Gist: Marion County commissioners approved a firefighter collective bargaining agreement that invests about $60 million over the life of the contract. The deal raises salaries by $20,000 and starts a transition to a 24-hours-on, 72-hours-off schedule by fall 2029.

Why It Matters: The contract changes both pay and shift structure for a countywide emergency service, with real budget stakes for Marion County.

Read more here.

🏥 New $15M SW 200 ER Breaks Ground

🏥 New $15M SW 200 ER Breaks Ground

The Gist: HCA Florida Healthcare broke ground May 27 on HCA Florida Canopy Oak Emergency at 8231 SW State Road 200 near On Top of the World. The $15 million, 11,000-square-foot freestanding ER will have 11 exam rooms, CT, X-ray and lab services, and is expected to employ 45 full-time healthcare professionals.

Why It Matters: The facility adds emergency capacity to the fast-growing State Road 200 corridor, where more residents are moving farther from Ocala's hospital core.

What's Next: HCA says the new emergency room is slated to open in spring 2027.
