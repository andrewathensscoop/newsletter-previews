🍗 Southern Plates in an 1890s Downtown Building

Ivy on the Square

At 53 S. Magnolia Avenue, Ivy on the Square is the downtown Ocala branch of the Ivy House family, serving Southern comfort food in a brick-and-iron building tied to the city's post-1883 fire rebuild.

The room leans into old Ocala, with tall ceilings, exposed brick, and historical photos. Order around the 30-year Baked Krispy Chicken recipe, shrimp and grits, a fried green tomato BLT, and the dessert bar.

🍽️ La Cuisine Closing After 17 Years

🍽️ La Cuisine Closing After 17 Years

The Gist: La Cuisine at 48 SW 1st Avenue will serve its last meal on June 26 after 17 years in downtown Ocala. Owners Elodie Guinard and Patrice Perron announced the sale, thanked customers for years of celebrations, and said a local downtown business owner has purchased the space and plans to keep it operating as a restaurant.

Why It Matters: Downtown loses one of its better-known dining anchors at the same time other longtime tenants have left, even as new hotels, a parking garage and new restaurants are moving into the core.

What's Next: Final service is June 26.

🐾 Pet Aquamation Vote Paused 60 Days

🐾 Pet Aquamation Vote Paused 60 Days

The Gist: Marion County commissioners unanimously tabled a special use permit for 60 days on June 17 after applicants Paula Dempsey and Ted Stone could not answer questions about operations and environmental impacts. They want to convert the former Orange Lake VFW Post 10733 at 19241 N U.S. Highway 441 into a Reddick facility using alkaline hydrolysis for deceased pets and small livestock.

Why It Matters: Neighbors are focused on wastewater, odor and how tightly the county would regulate a new animal aftercare use.

What's Next: The permit is expected back after the 60-day pause, sooner if applicants bring the missing operational data.

🐊 Rainbow River Reopens After Gator Bite

🐊 Rainbow River Reopens After Gator Bite

The Gist: A 19-year-old male snorkeler was bitten by an alligator on the Rainbow River near Sateke Village on June 21, then treated and released after transport to UF Health Shands. FWC said a contracted nuisance trapper removed the 8-foot-3-inch alligator, and the river reopened after the temporary closure.

Why It Matters: Rainbow River is one of Marion County's signature outdoor draws, so even a rare bite affects visitors, outfitters and residents who use the waterway.

What's Next: FWC says anyone concerned about an alligator should call 866-FWC-GATOR, 866-392-4286.

🚒 Evan Lewis Service Set for Friday

🚒 Evan Lewis Service Set for Friday

The Gist: Marion County Fire Rescue firefighter and EMT Evan Lewis died after an off-duty crash on June 14. Family, friends and coworkers gathered at Orlando Regional Medical Center for an Honor Walk recognizing his decision to be an organ donor.

Why It Matters: Lewis' death hits a county fire rescue agency already central to emergency response, and the Honor Walk gives residents a clear way to understand the loss beyond a crash report.

What's Next: The Celebration of Life is June 26 at 11 a.m. at First Baptist Church of Ocala.

🏍️ County Hits 10 Motorcycle Deaths

🏍️ County Hits 10 Motorcycle Deaths

The Gist: A 62-year-old Ocala man died after a motorcycle and pickup collided around 5:50 a.m. June 22 at County Road 475 and SE 178th Street. Ocala-News reported FHP said a 71-year-old Summerfield driver turned from a stop sign into the motorcycle's path, and the crash raised Marion County to at least 10 motorcycle-related deaths this year.

Why It Matters: The crash adds to a road-safety year already running ahead of 2025's fatality pace, with 42 traffic deaths reported before Monday's wreck.
