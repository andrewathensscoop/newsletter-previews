🍗 Soul Food on Meeting Street Road

At 2332 Meeting Street Road, Bertha's Kitchen is North Charleston's old-school soul food lunch counter, family-run since 1979. Order at the counter, find a seat, and expect the line to start building before lunch.

The regulars know the rhythm: fried pork chops, okra soup, lima beans over rice, and cornbread. The 2017 James Beard America's Classic nod is real, but the room still feels like a neighborhood place first.

🪦 Human Remains Found at Stadium Site

The Gist: A Johnson Hagood Stadium renovation crew found roughly 50 human remains Tuesday while working on the visitors' side grandstand. Charleston officials confirmed the discovery, and both The Citadel and the city said they have known about the burial site since 2004.

Why It Matters: The find puts a major Charleston institution, a known burial site and an active stadium project in the same public conversation about preservation, construction and how the city handles human remains.

What's Next: Officials said a community meeting is scheduled for Thursday, June 25, to discuss the findings.

🏘️ Lake Village Rezoning Moves Ahead

The Gist: The Berkeley County Planning Commission approved a rezoning request for Lake Village, a proposed 2,000-acre development near Cane Bay with up to 5,500 homes, restaurants, retail and a new public school. More than 20 people spoke at the meeting, with traffic, flooding and infrastructure among the biggest concerns.

Why It Matters: Cane Bay and the Highway 17-A corridor are already feeling the pressure of rapid growth, so this project would be one of the biggest new tests of Berkeley County's roads, drainage and school capacity.

What's Next: The rezoning still needs three readings in both the Land Use Committee and full County Council, plus a formal public hearing.

🏫 Dorchester Schools Eye 1% Tax

The Gist: Dorchester District 2 trustees approved a project list for a proposed 1% Education Capital Improvement Sales Tax that could go to voters in November and also support Dorchester District 4. DD2's list runs about $200 million to $300 million and includes a new middle school near Delmar Highway, high school upgrades, roofs, HVAC, buses, playgrounds and technology.

Why It Matters: Fast-growing Summerville-area schools are trying to close a capital gap with a tax that would also collect revenue from visitors and shoppers outside Dorchester County.

What's Next: Dorchester District 4 will submit its own project list, then Dorchester County Council will decide the tax lifespan and revenue split. Council's next meeting is July 6 in St. George.

Read more here.

🏠 Mount Pleasant Weighs 150% AMI Cap

The Gist: Mount Pleasant leaders are considering raising the income cap for buyers at Gregorie Ferry Towns from 80% to 150% of area median income, which is about $94,000. Some homeowners say the current cap makes it hard to sell, while housing advocate Denise Marie Fugo warned the change may not help the more than 5,000 residents who need housing assistance.

Why It Matters: The debate shows how hard it is to preserve workforce housing in Mount Pleasant when household incomes, home prices and resale rules no longer line up cleanly.

What's Next: Town Council is set for first reading on July 14, with a second reading and possible vote in August.

Read more here.

🗳️ SC-01 Runoffs Pick November Nominees

The Gist: Democrat Nancy Lacore and Republican Jenny Costa Honeycutt won Tuesday's primary runoffs for South Carolina's 1st Congressional District. Lacore is a retired three-star Navy rear admiral, and Honeycutt is a Charleston County Council member who said her campaign is focused on serving Lowcountry residents.

Why It Matters: The open seat gives Lowcountry voters a new House member while military, port, flooding and growth issues keep landing in Washington.

What's Next: Lacore and Honeycutt advance to the November general election for the SC-01 seat.
