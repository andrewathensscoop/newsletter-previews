🍗 Scratch-made soul food on Folly Road

Gillie's Seafood

At 805 Folly Road on James Island, Gillie's Seafood is chef Sean Mendes's scratch-made Lowcountry restaurant, modeled after the Sunday meals he remembers at his grandmother's house.

Fried chicken, shrimp and grits, collards, red rice, limas and Catfish Charleston fill the menu. It is warm and unfussy, with the kind of plates that call for extra napkins and no rush.

🚌 CARTA Plans Nearly $15M Upgrade

🚌 CARTA Plans Nearly $15M Upgrade

The Gist: CARTA's proposed budget includes nearly $15 million in added funding, much of it from federal transit grants. The plan calls for a new Shipwatch Square transit hub in North Charleston, upgrades downtown, a HOP park-and-ride lot and nearly $5 million for eight to 10 hybrid buses.

Why It Matters: The investment targets the reliability and waiting conditions riders deal with every day while adding newer buses that should require less maintenance.

🔥 Horse Island Fire Reaches 900 Acres

🔥 Horse Island Fire Reaches 900 Acres

The Gist: The Horse Island Fire in Francis Marion National Forest grew to an estimated 900 acres and reached 10% containment Tuesday. Ninety firefighters and two helicopters were assigned to the fire, and no evacuations were in effect.

Why It Matters: Heavy fuels are still smoldering near Shulerville, creating smoke and public-safety concerns even as crews gain confidence that the fire will stay inside established lines.

What's Next: Crews will keep patrolling and reinforcing the perimeter. Yellow Jacket, Horse Island and New Hope forest roads remain closed, and the cause is still under investigation.

💧 Septic Repair Aid Opens in Mount Pleasant

💧 Septic Repair Aid Opens in Mount Pleasant

The Gist: Mount Pleasant Waterworks and Charleston Waterkeeper launched financial assistance for failing septic systems, with repairs ranging from about $500 to $12,000. Applicants must use septic, lack public sewer access and meet federal low-income guidelines for the Charleston-North Charleston area.

Why It Matters: Failing tanks can send bacteria into nearby waterways, creating health risks for residents who swim, paddle, fish or harvest oysters.

What's Next: Applications are open at no cost. Mount Pleasant Waterworks will screen household income, then schedule septic inspections for eligible applicants.

⛵ Coast Faces 400-Boat Derelict Risk

⛵ Coast Faces 400-Boat Derelict Risk

The Gist: Marina operators, state agencies and lawmakers met in Charleston to address as many as 400 aging boats that could lose insurance and end up abandoned in creeks and marshes. Rep. Tom Hartnett's earlier removal bill passed the state House 115-0 but died without a Senate hearing.

Why It Matters: Neglected boats can sink, leak fuel, block waterways and leave marinas or taxpayers responsible for expensive removal after years of coastal cleanup work.

What's Next: Hartnett and the group plan to draft new abandoned-vessel legislation for the next state legislative session.

Read more here.

🏛️ Gullah Geechee Corridor Wins $150K

🏛️ Gullah Geechee Corridor Wins $150K

The Gist: The Gullah Geechee Cultural Heritage Corridor received a $150,000 National Trust for Historic Preservation grant to fund a new preservation director. The Charleston-based corridor was one of 27 sites sharing $3 million and says the role will support hundreds of organizations across four states.

Why It Matters: The position gives Lowcountry communities more help documenting historic places, finding grants and responding when development or public works threaten traditional Gullah Geechee spaces.

What's Next: An early priority is building a physical and digital archive of documents and research, while the new director will also coordinate with planners, developers and federal engineers.
