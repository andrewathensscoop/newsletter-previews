Chubby Fish

The room is small, walk-in only, and built for people willing to wait for the board to shift. Expect Lowcountry seafood with a chef's hand on it, from raw fish and oysters to plates that follow the day's catch.

🌊 Folly Reopens Its 10-Year Plan

🌊 Folly Reopens Its 10-Year Plan

The Gist: Folly Beach is updating its comprehensive plan for the first time since 2015, with stormwater management, business development, quality of life and resilience on the table. Mayor Chris Bizzell said pedestrian safety is already one of the leading issues, including which roads and accident hot spots should be handled first.

Why It Matters: The plan will shape how Folly spends limited resources on flooding, growth and beach-town livability for the next decade, so this is one of the more direct chances residents have to steer the island's priorities.

What's Next: An online survey is open now, the first public meeting is scheduled for Aug. 3 at City Hall, and the public input period runs through Sept. 7.

🏢 West Ashley Office Project Starts Fencing

🏢 West Ashley Office Project Starts Fencing

The Gist: Charleston has started installing security fencing ahead of demolition at the former Catholic Diocese office near Ashley Landing. The city plans to turn the long-vacant property into a consolidated city office building with room for 150 to 175 employees.

Why It Matters: The project shifts more city operations into West Ashley and puts a dormant site near a major shopping area back into civic use, which matters for residents who have long argued city services are too peninsula-centered.

What's Next: Demolition is expected to begin next week, and the city says the project should be finished in about 18 months.

🏗️ North Charleston Starts Zoning Rewrite

🏗️ North Charleston Starts Zoning Rewrite

The Gist: North Charleston is launching One North Charleston, a Unified Development Ordinance effort that would consolidate and rewrite the city's development rules. Planning director Tim Macholl said the current zoning ordinance dates to 1974, and city staff expects North Charleston to add about 30,000 residents over the next 10 to 15 years.

Why It Matters: This rewrite will help decide what can be built, where growth goes and how development lines up with the Lowcountry Rapid Transit corridor, Rivers Avenue and neighborhood quality of life.

What's Next: The ordinance will go through committees, councils and boards with public outreach, and Macholl said he hopes it can be adopted by summer 2027.

🚦 Mount Pleasant Targets Busy Intersection

🚦 Mount Pleasant Targets Busy Intersection

The Gist: Mount Pleasant's Bids and Purchases Committee is considering a $133,720.68 contract with HDR Engineering Inc. of the Carolinas to design improvements at Venning Road and Hungryneck Boulevard. The work would include surveys, construction plans, traffic signal design, drainage calculations, stormwater planning and permitting.

Why It Matters: Town officials say Midtown growth has added pressure at the intersection, where right-turning drivers now share lanes with through traffic and backups build on Venning Road.

What's Next: If approved, the design and permitting phase would begin before construction, with the design process expected to take about 12 months.

🗳️ County Tweaks $4.3B Tax Ballot

🗳️ County Tweaks $4.3B Tax Ballot

The Gist: Charleston County Council voted June 25 to change wording on a proposed $4.3 billion transportation sales tax referendum for November. The revised language says funds will include specific listed projects, including $445 million for U.S. 17 South widening in West Ashley, $230 million for Johnnie Dodds and Houston Northcutt improvements and $225 million for the Battery Extension seawall.

Why It Matters: The ballot language is part of the county's second attempt to sell voters on a long-term half-cent tax after the 2024 referendum failed, with roads, transit and greenbelt funding all competing for trust.

What's Next: The proposal is planned for the November ballot, and the new tax would replace the existing 2004 half-cent sales tax after current collections end in 2027.
