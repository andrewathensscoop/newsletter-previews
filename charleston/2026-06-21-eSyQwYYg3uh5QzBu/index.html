Oysters on Bowens Island Road

At 1870 Bowens Island Road, Bowens Island Restaurant is the no-frills oyster joint tucked between James Island and Folly Beach. The menu keeps it simple: steamed oysters, fried seafood, Frogmore stew, local drafts, and cocktails.

The place feels more like a Lowcountry ritual than a polished dining room. May Bowen started it in 1946, and Robert Barber accepted a James Beard America's Classics award there in 2006.

🗳️ Runoff Election Day Is Tuesday

The Gist: South Carolina's runoff elections are Tuesday, June 23, after no candidate cleared 50% in several June 9 primaries. Charleston County elections director Isaac Cramer said more than 600 people had voted by midday Wednesday, with Mount Pleasant the busiest early voting site.

Why It Matters: Lowcountry ballots include the Republican races for governor, attorney general, agriculture commissioner and SC-1, plus the Democratic SC-1 runoff in areas including Mount Pleasant, James Island, Folly Beach, Johns Island, Kiawah and Seabrook.

What's Next: Polls are open from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. Tuesday, June 23. Absentee ballots must reach the Charleston County Board of Voter Registration and Elections by 7 p.m. that day.

Read more here.

🏥 MUSC, Cigna Deadline Nears

The Gist: MUSC Health and Cigna Healthcare are still negotiating as their current agreement nears expiration at the end of June. MUSC says Cigna wants major payment changes, while Cigna says it has proposed rate increases close to what MUSC requested and wants stronger cost guardrails.

Why It Matters: If the two sides miss the deadline, Cigna members who use MUSC could face harder choices on doctors, hospitals and out-of-network costs across Charleston's largest academic health system.

What's Next: MUSC says patients receiving care should keep scheduled appointments while negotiations continue.

Read more here.

🌊 Folly Weighs Beach Preservation Fund

The Gist: Folly Beach is discussing beach preservation as part of its fiscal year 2027 budget. Mayor Chris Bizzell said about 60% of the city's budget comes from local accommodations taxes, and the city is putting about $1 million into a beach preservation fund for renourishment and storm protection.

Why It Matters: For Folly, dunes and renourishment are not cosmetic. They protect homes, roads, tourism revenue and evacuation access when storms push water across the island.

What's Next: City officials are scheduled to discuss the budget Tuesday, June 23, at 3 p.m.

Read more here.

🏛️ Hanahan Ethics Case Awaits Ruling

The Gist: The South Carolina Ethics Commission heard hours of testimony on whether Hanahan Mayor Christie Rainwater violated ethics laws tied to a city-owned property sale on Yeamans Hall Road. Staff pointed to use-of-office and failure-to-recuse allegations after a roughly $12,750 Carolina One commission was corrected before closing.

Why It Matters: The case turns on public trust around city land deals, outside business ties and when elected officials have to step back from municipal transactions.

What's Next: Commissioners did not vote Thursday and are expected to issue a written ruling in the coming weeks.

Read more here.

🍤 Seewee Listed For Nearly $2.5M

The Gist: Seewee Restaurant at 4808 North Highway 17 in Awendaw is listed for nearly $2.5 million. The building dates to 1929, Mary Rancourt turned it into a restaurant in 1993, and general manager Tim Penninger says the family is selling for retirement reasons, not because the restaurant is closing.

Why It Matters: Seewee is one of those roadside Lowcountry institutions people treat like a landmark, so a sale raises the real question of whether a new owner preserves the seafood spot or changes a familiar piece of Highway 17.

What's Next: The restaurant remains open while the property is listed with National Restaurant Properties, and no redevelopment plans have been announced.
